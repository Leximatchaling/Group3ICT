Statement of the Problem

1. How do hydrodynamic variables such as water velocity, shear stress, and turbulence mathematically correlate with the nest-site selection patterns of janitor fish in the Marikina River?
2. What is the spatial distribution of janitor fish burrows across selected river sections based on derived hydrodynamic and sediment-related measurements?
3. How do temporal changes in flow conditions affect sediment cohesion and the predicted structural stability of existing burrows using mathematical or simulation-based modeling?
4. What spatial or physical patterns such as zones of high scouring potential, clustering trends, or directional nest alignment can be identified through computational hydrodynamic mapping and geomorphological analysis?
