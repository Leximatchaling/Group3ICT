Research objectives

Effective communication of hazards during floods, earthquakes, and typhoons is important to minimize risks to human life. Current visual alerts, such as LED hazard signals, are often insufficient for individuals with visual impairments or in low-visibility conditions. To address this gap, this study proposes the development of AURA-BEACON, an Arduino-based unified reality audio beacon designed to translate LED hazard signals into real-time audio alerts. The device uses various environmental sensors to detect hazards such as rising water levels, smoke, heat, or vibrations and immediately triggers pre-recorded audio warnings. AURA-BEACON aims to enhance accessibility, situational awareness, and community responsiveness during calamities, supporting both vulnerable populations and disaster response personnel. The prototype will be tested under varying conditions to ensure accurate detection and clear audio playback. By combining visual and audio alerts, the system seeks to improve emergency response and provide a better foundation for more inclusive early-warning systems.
